<p><p>Flue Gas Desulfurization (FGD) is a set of technologies aimed at reducing sulfur dioxide (SO2) emissions from industrial processes, particularly from power plants that burn fossil fuels. FGD systems typically involve the use of a sorbent, such as limestone, which reacts with the SO2 in flue gas to produce gypsum. This process is vital for meeting environmental regulations and reducing air pollution, thus playing a crucial role in promoting cleaner air and environmental sustainability.</p><p>The FGD market is poised for significant growth, driven by increasing regulatory pressures and the global shift towards cleaner energy sources. Industries are increasingly adopting FGD technologies to comply with stringent emission standards and to enhance operational efficiency.
