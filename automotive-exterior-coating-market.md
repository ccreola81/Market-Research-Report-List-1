<p><p>The automotive exterior coating market is primarily divided into water-based and oil-based paints. Water-based paints utilize water as a solvent, resulting in lower volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions, making them environmentally friendly and increasingly popular due to stringent regulations. Oil-based paints, on the other hand, contain higher levels of solvents, providing a durable finish suitable for various weather conditions, though their VOC emissions are higher. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and environmental standards in the automotive industry.</p></p>
