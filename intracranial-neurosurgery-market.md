Craniotomy involves temporarily removing a portion of the skull to access the brain for procedures like tumor removal or aneurysm repair. Craniectomy entails the permanent removal of a skull section, often to alleviate pressure or treat severe trauma. Cranioplasty is the reconstruction of the skull after injury or surgery. Other procedures include stereotactic surgery and endoscopic techniques, expanding treatment options for neurological conditions.</p></p>
