<p><p>The High Voltage Direct Current (HVDC) transmission system market is primarily divided into three types: Line Commutated Converter (LCC), Voltage Source Converter (VSC), and Others. LCC technology is efficient for long-distance transmission but requires synchronous sources, making it suitable for bulk power transfer. VSC, on the other hand, enables flexible control, connection of renewable sources, and operation on weak grids. The "Others" category includes emerging technologies, innovations, and hybrid systems that enhance transmission efficiency and reliability.</p></p>
