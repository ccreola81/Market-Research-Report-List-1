<p><p>Leaf wetness sensors are crucial in agriculture for monitoring moisture levels on plant surfaces. The market can be categorized into bionic and common types. Bionic sensors mimic natural processes, utilizing biological principles to provide accurate moisture readings, often integrating advanced technologies for improved sensitivity. Common leaf wetness sensors, on the other hand, typically employ electrical or optical methods for moisture detection. Both types enhance crop management by informing irrigation practices and disease risk assessments, ultimately optimizing agricultural productivity.</p></p>
