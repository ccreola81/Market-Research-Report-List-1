<p><p>The polymers used in medical devices include various types, each offering unique properties. PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride) is known for its flexibility and durability, making it ideal for tubing and bags. PP (Polypropylene) is lightweight and resistant to chemicals, often used in syringes and containers. PS (Polystyrene) provides clarity and rigidity, suitable for trays and packaging. PE (Polyethylene) is versatile and resistant to moisture, commonly used in bags and containers. TPE (Thermoplastic Elastomer) combines rubber-like flexibility with thermoplastic processing, widely used in grips and seals. Other polymers may include materials like polycarbonate or nylon, catering to specific device requirements.</p></p>
