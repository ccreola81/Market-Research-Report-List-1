<p><p>The solar salt market primarily consists of two types: sea water and underground brine. Sea water salt is derived from the evaporation of seawater in large, shallow ponds, harnessing solar energy to crystallize salt. This method is widely used due to its cost-effectiveness and abundance of supply. On the other hand, underground brine salt is obtained from salt deposits found underground, where natural brine is pumped to the surface for evaporation. Both sources cater to various industrial and consumer demand while influencing pricing and availability.</p></p>
