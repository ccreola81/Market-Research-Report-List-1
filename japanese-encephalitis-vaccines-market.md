<p><p>The Japanese Encephalitis (JE) vaccines market comprises two primary types: attenuated vaccines and inactivated vaccines. Attenuated vaccines use weakened live viruses to stimulate an immune response without causing disease, offering robust protection and long-lasting immunity. In contrast, inactivated vaccines contain killed viruses, ensuring safety while still inducing an immune response, though they may require booster doses for sustained protection. Both types are essential for preventing JE, particularly in endemic regions, contributing to public health efforts against this viral infection.</p></p>
