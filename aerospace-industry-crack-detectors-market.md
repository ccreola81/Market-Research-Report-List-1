<p><p>The aerospace industry employs various crack detection methods to ensure structural integrity and safety. Eddy current testing utilizes electromagnetic induction to detect surface and near-surface cracks in conductive materials. Dielectric testing assesses insulation and structural integrity by measuring changes in capacitance. Imaging techniques, including X-ray and ultrasound, provide visual representations of internal defects. Phased array technology uses multiple ultrasonic elements to scan and visualize potential crack locations, offering detailed and accurate assessments of material conditions. Each method serves a critical role in maintenance and quality assurance.</p></p>
