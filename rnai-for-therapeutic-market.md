<p><p>RNA interference (RNAi) is a biological process used in therapeutic applications to silence specific genes and regulate gene expression. The therapeutic market for RNAi primarily consists of three types: small interfering RNA (siRNA), which directly targets and degrades mRNA; microRNA (miRNA), which modulates gene expression by targeting multiple mRNAs; and short hairpin RNA (shRNA), which is processed into siRNA for sustained gene silencing. These RNAi technologies are promising for treating various diseases, including cancers and genetic disorders.</p></p>
